The Museum of Vineyard and Wine, from Harlau

Address: 7, Logofat Tautu Street, Harlau, Iaşi
Email: muzeulvinului @yahoo.com
Tel: 0040.230.2720.866
Program: 9:00-17:00, Monday-closed.

The Museum of Vineyard and Wine, from Harlau, is situated in a 19th Century building, known as the Mansion of Boyar Tautu. This Museum, unique in the Moldova Region, houses an ethnography collection from the vineyard subject area. On exhibit are the most important moments, in which the wine is consumed by Romanian peasants, specific tools for obtaining the wine, also unusual aspects immortalized on documentary photographs made during the research related to the exhibition.

Cotnari Winery

Cotnari commune, Iasi county
Tel.: 0040.232.730.393
Fax: 0040.232.730.205
Website: www.cotnari.ro contact@cotnari.ro office@cotnari.ro

It is said that the toponym "Cotnari" comes from an old handicraft, the construction of the barells: "cot" (Romanian word) = "ell" an old unit of measurement = approx. 0.60 m; "ar" a suffix for the nouns indicating an occupation in the word "cotinar" = the handicraftsman of the barells. Another interpretation is that "Cotnari", as a toponym, refers to the inhabitants (cotunari/catunari) of a very limited rural area, a "hamlet" = "cotun"/"catun". (cf. Vladimir Palamariu, "The oiconyms from Moldavia", 2002).

Ruginoasa Palace

Cotnari commune, Iasi county
Tel.: 0040.232.730.393
Fax: 0040.232.730.205
Website: www.cotnari.ro contact@cotnari.ro office@cotnari.ro

Residence of Prince Al.I.Cuza, the palace was built at the beginning of the 19th century, by the treasurer Sandulache Sturdza. The architect of the luxurious building was the Viennese Johann Freywald. The Neoclassical aspect of the edifice was changed in 1847, when chancellor Costache Sturdza charged architect Johann Brandel, with the restoration of the house in Ruginoasa, following the Gothic style architecture. Destroyed during World War II, the palace was restored beginning with 1978.

Nova Nautic Ezareni Recreation Area

Address: Ezareni, Iaşi, Romania
Tel: 0040.740.190.706; 0040.788.325.320
E-mail: contact@ezareni.com
web: www.ezareni.com

After exiting Iasi, to the South West, situated on the right side of the road to Horpaz, there is the ? Ezareni Amusement Resort. Located on a private property, the resort covers 3 hectares (7.05 acres) and is open to the Ezareni basin, which covers a total area of 47 hectares (116 acres). It can be reached by car and it has a secure parking. The resort includes a restaurant with fish specialties (150 seats), a terrace with barbecue (200 seats) and accommodation. Leisure possible: leisure rides by motor or rowing boats and hydro-bicycles. The pontoon and?the lake shores provide opportunities for sun bathing, the beach being furnished with lounge chairs, umbrellas and children' pool.

The area is accessible by vehicle, its Tatarasi be from the South (in the "Doi Băieți" following river alley) or Airport Road (from Eternity Street, near the Iron Bridge). In addition to these amenities, the area also has a golf Ciric mini golf, children's play parks (with area inflatable), cycle rides and pedalos on the lake (4 pantoane) and boats.

Barnova Monastery

Patron: Saint George (April, 23-rd)-from the wooden church.
Address: Barnova village, Iaşi


This monastery is located close to the city, in a dense forest. It was built by the Moldavian rulers Miron Barnovschi and Eustratie Dabija, between 1626 and 1666. Its origins lie in a wooden church, built in 1603, by a ruler of Moldavia, Ieremia Movilă. The plan of the church was initially similar to the one of the Dragomirna Monastery, but the final result is much different than the original. The monastic complex was included in 2004 on the list of the historical monuments in Iasi county and it consists of the following: the church, the cells, the ruins of Lady Dafina Dabija's palace, the gate tower and the interior wall. Inside, on the right wall there are two tombstones: the one belonging to the Dabija ruler, beheaded in 1666, in Istambul and another one, of his daughter, Maria, who died at the age of 15.
