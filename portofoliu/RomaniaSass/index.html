Pescatourism in Romania (Mila 23, Danube Delta)

× Pescatourism is a form of ecotourism and responsible tourism, cultural, done with care for the environment. The "Lotca Habitat: Pescaturism in the Danube Delta" initiated by Ivan Patzaichin, multiple Olympic champion canoe, originally from Mile 23. Special pescatourism traveled routes are guided into the dinghy specially built for tourism, elegant and comfortable, based on the tradition “marangoz”, builders of boats in the Delta. The concept assumes that the tourist to live a day with authentic fishermen in a traditional fisherman household Mila 23, and where have the opportunity to experience unique, original, culture and gastronomy fisherman. Legends fishery will enrich the journey and the old recipes of cooks in Mile 23, will complete the trip with the surprising ways to cook fish. This type of tourist activity, enriches and diversifies delta tourism offer, added new experience to tourists who want to approach the culture of the place and nature.

Nordik - Walking in Romania

× Nordic Walking appeared in Finland at the beginning of the last century when finish skiers started using wood sticks during summer training. This sport was “rediscovered” in 1992 in the U.S.A. (Pole-Walking). In a short period of time the amazing results of this sport launched at the end of the ’90 a real trend not only in Finland. Due to its benefits, Nordic Walking has become a widely-practiced sport especially in Austria, Germany and of course the Scandinavian Countries. Today, Nordic Walking is the sport with the biggest growing popularity in Western Europe. This sport brings a lot of health benefits and can be practiced by people of all ages including those with health problems. More and more health insurance companies encourage this sport and reimburse the costs of its practice. In Vatra Dornei there are 45 km of trails. R1-R4 trails are dedicated to Nordic Walking and R5 and R6 for Mountain Biking.

TRANSROMANICA

× Transromanica explores the Romanesque common heritage of Europe (cathedrals, monasteries and medieval churches) in order to promote and appreciate the development of Romanesque heritage sites for cultural and touristic purposes, thus supporting regional and economic development. Part of the Transromanica European Cultural Route certified by the Council of Europe is The Roman Catholic Cathedral “St. Michael’s” (Alba Iulia, Alba County), the oldest and longest cathedral in Romania. But the richness of national heritage is not limited to it. There are other objectives with major potential for inclusion in the Route, including The “Citadel” Complex (Burgviertel) (Câlnic village, Câlnic commune, Alba County), 13th century; Feldioara Citadel (Feldioara commune, Brașov County), 13th century; Deva Medieval Citadel (Deva, Hunedoara County), 13th century, construction that guarded for centuries the entry into Transylvania on the Mureș valley. To these are added two housing towers (Donjon): Gârbova Gârbova commune, Alba County) dating back to the 13th century and Cheresig (Toboliu commune, Bihor County) built in the 13th century.
